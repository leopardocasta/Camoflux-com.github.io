[ 02 / DESCRIPTION ]
SHORT — 50 WORDS

Camoflux is a hand-painted episodic exploration game where landscapes, bodies, and technology fuse. As The Other, channel circuits of environmental energy, camouflage into shifting terrains, and reshape a cyclical cataclysm — through play, not conquest.

LONG — 150 WORDS

Camoflux: Levels & Bosses is a third-person exploration game built across four episodic chapters. You inhabit The Other, a being who adapts to changing environments through vibrational terraforming, camouflage, and electromagnetic sensors. Glide through interconnected hand-painted worlds where landscapes, bodies, and technology share sentience.

A unique combat system lets you destroy bosses or dynamically engage with them — refusing the binary that defines most action games. Customize through camouflage. Switch embodiments. Solve surreal environmental puzzles in landscapes built from Romantic and Surrealist painting, VR sculpting, and Unreal Engine 5.

Camoflux is currently exhibited at the 2026 Whitney Biennial as a playable installation. Steam release: 2026.

[ 07 / STUDIO ]

About Levels & Bosses

Levels & Bosses is an independent studio led by Leo Castañeda (b. 1988, Cali, Colombia), working at the intersection of fine art, philosophy, and game design. The studio's practice treats painting, 3D modeling, and game engine work as co-equal forms of making.

Castañeda's work has been shown at the Whitney Museum of American Art, Haus der Elektronischen Künste Basel, and Rhizome, and is held in the Whitney's permanent collection. His phrase third-person ontology describes a methodology where world-formation operates as philosophical proposition rather than genre convention.

Camoflux is published by Otro Inventario.
